Top insight

Experience and SEO health tell 2 different stories in the same audit.

Shoppers feel a 22 average lab experience while SEO health reads 67. That gap is revenue and brand risk when traffic slips.
Demand

Organic visits are down about 12 percent year over year.

Seasonality still moves the line, but the year-over-year slope is not your friend when branded share is this high.
Concentration

More than 9 in 10 organic visits still come from branded queries.

That is efficient until volume weakens. You need a deliberate non-branded plan before you feel the hit in revenue.
AI visibility

Roughly 3.84 M sampled citations exist, but they skew hard to one surface.

Google AI Mode dominates the split. Visibility is real; diversification of answers and prompts is the unlock.
Capital

The same 4 templates carry most of the story and most of the pain.

Women’s, homepage, Jordan, and Nike By You cluster in the twenties while Women’s collapses in lab. Fund them as one program.
Recommendations

Run the flagship program as 1 portfolio, not 4 projects.

Capital and governance choices that keep experience, SEO, and speed moving together.
  • Fund the four templates under one program
    Align Women’s, homepage, Jordan, and Nike By You under shared component, tag, and media rules so each fix compounds instead of competing for the same engineers.
  • Use one executive scorecard each quarter
    Review lab experience, SEO health, organic trend, and AI citation mix in the same forum so tradeoffs stay visible and no single metric hides risk.
Recommendations

Plan for weaker organic before branded efficiency stops masking it.

Demand resilience when concentration is this high.
Branded share above nine in ten is efficient until volume slips. Make the downside visible early.
  • Set a public non-branded growth bar
    Name a target band for non-branded organic contribution with marketing and finance so the team invests before concentration shows up as a revenue surprise.
  • Stress-test revenue to continued organic decline
    Model what happens if the year-over-year slope on the organic line continues so plans do not assume last year’s visit levels.
Recommendations

Treat AI visibility as a managed channel, not a side metric.

Ownership and monitoring when citations skew to one surface.
  • Assign a single owner for assistant answers
    Give one leader accountability for structured product facts, FAQs, and landing consistency so sampled citations stay accurate as assortments change.
  • Broaden what you monitor beyond the dominant surface
    Track prompts and outcomes across assistants so you catch drift when share shifts away from today’s largest slice.
